Proportional Allocation

Allocate sample size proportionally across population strata

Formula

n_i = (N_i / N) × n
Where n_i is the sample size for stratum i, N_i is the population of stratum i, N is the total population, and n is the total sample size. This ensures each stratum is represented proportionally to its size in the population.

Taro Yamane Formula

Calculate sample size for finite populations

Formula

n = N / (1 + N × e²)
Where n is the sample size, N is the population size, and e is the margin of error. This formula assumes a 95% confidence level (Z = 1.96) and is suitable for finite populations where the sample will be a significant proportion of the population.

Cochran's Formula

Sample size calculation for proportions with flexible input formats

Formula

n₀ = Z² × p × (1-p) / e²
For finite populations: n = n₀ / (1 + (n₀ - 1)/N)

Where Z is the Z-score for the confidence level, p is the estimated proportion, e is the margin of error, and N is the population size (optional for finite population correction). Accepts multiple input formats: 0.50, 50, or 50%.
Accepts: 0.50 (decimal), 50 (percentage), or 50% (with symbol)
